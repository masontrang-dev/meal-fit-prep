Fish
🐟
Fish
4–5 meals · 2 people · ~3–3.5 lbs/week
Buy: Frozen salmon fillets, tilapia, cod, or mahi-mahi. Individually vacuum-sealed — thaw overnight in fridge. No scaling, no deboning on standard fillets. Costco frozen salmon bag is best value. Trader Joe's individual fillets are convenient.

Eat earlier in the week — cooked fish keeps only 3–4 days. Mon–Wed lunches and dinners are ideal.
Cooking: Pat dry, season (see Sauces tab), bake at 400°F for 12–15 min on foil-lined sheet pan. Don't crowd the pan.

Never reheated on high heat — 50% microwave power, 90 sec. Or oven at 275°F covered with foil for 10 min.
~30g protein / 6ozOmega-3s
Chicken
🍗
Thighs
Best for Sunday batch
Why thighs for prep: Higher fat content keeps them moist when reheated. Much harder to overcook than breast. Flavor holds better through day 4–5 in the fridge.

Amount: ~2–2.5 lbs for Sunday batch. Reserve ~1 lb separately for Wednesday's cast iron marinade.

Cooking: 375°F for 35–40 min. Season well and they're nearly impossible to ruin.
~30g protein / 4ozMost forgiving
🍗
Breast
Best for cast iron or fresh cook
Why breast for cast iron: Leaner (better for weight loss goals), great when eaten fresh off the pan — no reheating dryness issue.

Prep tips if batch cooking: Pound to even thickness before baking. Pull at exactly 165°F. Store with a little sauce in the container. Reheat at 50% microwave power only.

Optional: One rotisserie chicken (~$7–8) = 4–5 shredded portions instantly for Mon–Tue lunches.

Steak
🥩
Steak — Always Cast Iron, Never Oven
2–3 meals · 2 people · 1.5–2 lbs/week
Buy: Flank or sirloin — leaner cuts, excellent sliced thin over bowls. One 1.5–2 lb flank steak = 4–6 portions for two.

Why always cast iron: The high dry heat of a cast iron creates the Maillard reaction crust that makes steak taste like steak. An oven can't replicate this on a thin cut. Flank and sirloin are ideal for the cast iron method.
Strategy: Marinate Sunday (use a Sunday-safe marinade — see Cast Iron tab) and cook Wednesday for peak freshness and flavor. Leftovers serve as Thursday lunch.

Cook to medium — not well done. A proper sear is fine; heavily blackened/charred is what to avoid. Rest 5 min before slicing.
~32g protein / 4ozIron + zinc

Saturday Night
Two-Minute Saturday Setup
Move frozen fish fillets from freezer to fridge — perfectly thawed by Sunday afternoon. If making refried pinto beans this week, cover 1 cup dried beans in water and soak overnight.
1
0 min · Start
Rice Cooker On + Oven Preheat + Chop Everything
Start brown rice in rice cooker first — 45 min, fully hands-off. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line two sheet pans with foil.

Chop all aromatics in one session now:
  • Dice 1 large onion — half goes into lentils, half into Wednesday's cast iron container
  • Mince 5–6 garlic cloves — divide same way
  • Slice bell peppers into strips for Wednesday
  • Slice mushrooms for Wednesday
Store Wednesday vegetables (onion half, garlic half, bell pepper strips, mushrooms) in a labeled container in the fridge. You won't touch them again until Wednesday.
2
15 min · 2 minutes active
Wednesday Marinade — Do It Now
Set aside ~1 lb chicken thighs or steak for Wednesday. Mix your chosen marinade (see Cast Iron tab). Pour over protein in a zip-lock bag, squeeze out air, label "Wednesday," refrigerate.

Safe to marinate from Sunday: Soy Garlic Ginger, Smoked Paprika Garlic — oil and soy-based, no significant acid, flavor only improves over 2–5 days.

Marinate Tuesday instead if choosing: Lime Cumin or Balsamic Herb — citrus and vinegar-based acids will break down protein texture if left more than 1–2 days.
3
18 min · Active
Season All Proteins for Sunday Batch
Fish (~3 lbs): Pat completely dry with paper towel — critical for good baking. Coat lightly with olive oil, season (see Sauces tab). Flat on foil-lined sheet pan, don't crowd.

Chicken thighs (~2–2.5 lbs): Season with olive oil + Italian herbs + minced garlic + salt + pepper. Place on second sheet pan alongside broccoli florets tossed in olive oil + salt.

Quinoa: Rinse in fine mesh strainer under cold water for 30 seconds. Add to rice cooker with 1:1.5 ratio water after brown rice finishes, or cook simultaneously on stovetop (15 min).
4
28 min · Hands Off
Everything in the Oven + Lentils on Stovetop
Fish: 12–15 min. Chicken + broccoli: 35–40 min. Set timers and step away.

While oven runs — start lentils: In a medium pot, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium. Sauté the lentil-portion of diced onion and garlic 3 min until soft. Add 1.5 cups dry lentils + 3 cups low-sodium broth + 1 tsp cumin + ½ tsp salt. Bring to boil, reduce to low, cover and simmer 22 min. They'll be done around the same time as the chicken.
5
65 min · Active
Pull, Rest, Cool, Portion
Fish comes out first. Chicken + broccoli a few minutes later. Lentils come off the stovetop.

Critical: let everything cool 10 minutes before lidding containers. Sealing hot food traps steam and makes everything soggy and unappealing by Monday.

Portion into containers — 2 portions per container (one lunch for both of you) or individual containers if preferred. Label by day or protein type. Brown rice and quinoa into their own containers. Lentils into a separate container.

Emergency Week — When Sunday Prep Doesn't Happen
Life happens. If you miss a Sunday, these five meals require no prep and can be assembled in 10 minutes or less from pantry staples and a quick grocery stop.
01
Rotisserie Chicken + Bagged Salad
Buy a whole rotisserie chicken (~$7). Shred over a pre-washed salad kit. Add canned black beans. Done in 5 minutes, zero cooking.
02
Canned Tuna + Rice + Frozen Veg
Drain canned tuna, season with olive oil + lemon + salt. Rice cooker on. Steam frozen veg bag in microwave 4 min. Complete meal in 20 min hands-off.
03
Frozen Fish + Microwave Sweet Potato
Thaw frozen fillet in cold water 30 min. Season and bake 15 min at 400°F. Microwave sweet potato 5 min simultaneously. Two ingredients, no prep.
04
Eggs + Black Beans + Rice
Scramble 3 eggs. Warm canned black beans with cumin. Rice cooker or microwave rice. High protein, zero shopping needed if pantry is stocked.
05
One-Pot Lentil Soup
Sauté onion + garlic 3 min. Add 1 cup dry lentils + 3 cups broth + diced canned tomatoes + cumin + smoked paprika. Simmer 25 min. Serves 2 generously with no other sides needed. Keeps 5 days — this becomes tomorrow's lunch too.

🔥 Wednesday Cast Iron Night
Everything prepped Sunday · 13 minutes to cook Wednesday · Fresh dinner mid-week
What you do on Sunday (5 min total):
  1. Choose protein: ~1 lb chicken thighs, chicken breast, or flank steak (feeds 2)
  2. Mix marinade of choice (see below — 1 minute)
  3. Pour over protein in zip-lock bag, squeeze out air, label "Wednesday," refrigerate
  4. Slice bell peppers, mushrooms, and onion — store in separate labeled container in fridge

What you do on Wednesday (13 min):
  1. Pull marinated protein and vegetable container from fridge
  2. Pat protein lightly dry with paper towel — improves browning
  3. Heat cast iron on high 2 minutes until just beginning to smoke
  4. Add a small drizzle of avocado oil — high smoke point, ideal for cast iron
  5. Chicken thighs or breast: 4–5 min per side. Flank steak: 3–4 min per side. Don't move it — let the crust form
  6. Last 2 minutes: add the pre-sliced veg around the protein — they pick up all the pan flavor
  7. Rest 3–5 min before slicing. Don't skip this — it keeps the juices inside
  8. Serve over quinoa or brown rice (already made from Sunday)

Steak is always cast iron — never oven. A thin cut like flank or sirloin needs the intense dry heat of cast iron to develop a proper crust. The oven produces a very different (inferior) result for steak.
Marinade Options — Rotate Week to Week
Sunday-safe marinades: Soy Garlic Ginger and Smoked Paprika Garlic are oil and soy-based with no significant acid — they can marinate safely for 2–5 days and actually improve with time. Tuesday-only marinades: Lime Cumin and Balsamic Herb contain citrus juice and vinegar — acids break down protein texture if left more than 1–2 days on chicken (slightly longer on steak).
Soy Garlic Ginger
Chicken thighs or breast · Best all-around
✓ Sunday Safe
Ingredients (feeds 2)
  • 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • Cracked black pepper to taste
No significant acid — safe from Sunday through Wednesday. Flavor deepens over time. The smell when it hits a hot cast iron is exceptional.
Smoked Paprika Garlic
Flank steak · Bold and simple
✓ Sunday Safe
Ingredients (feeds 2)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp cracked black pepper
Oil-based rub with no acid — safe from Sunday. Rub into steak well on both sides. Excellent sliced thin over lentils or quinoa.
Lime Cumin
Chicken or steak · Mexican bowl style
Tuesday Only

PKD (Polycystic Kidney Disease) — Important: This plan is built on whole foods and aligns directionally with DASH principles. However, PKD dietary needs vary significantly by kidney function stage (GFR). Potassium, phosphorus, and total daily protein all need calibration to your specific labs. Before committing to this plan, bring it printed to your nephrologist or a renal dietitian. Ask specifically about your current GFR, your potassium and phosphorus tolerance, and whether your protein target needs adjusting. The structure here is sound — the individual quantities may need tuning.
DASH Diet Alignment
DASH emphasizes lean proteins, whole grains, vegetables, and reduced sodium. This plan aligns well with two key practices: (1) use low-sodium soy sauce throughout — regular soy sauce has ~900mg sodium per tablespoon, low-sodium cuts that roughly in half, coconut aminos cuts it further. (2) Season with herbs and citrus rather than salt wherever possible. Target under 1,500–2,300mg total sodium daily.

How This Supports Your Fitness Plan
Post-workout eating: Eat a prepped container within 30–60 minutes after treadmill or strength sessions. Protein supports muscle repair; the complex carbs replenish energy stores. Any container from this plan works perfectly.
Pre-workout fuel: Eat a grain-containing meal 1–2 hours before a session. Brown rice, quinoa, or lentils all provide sustained energy without a spike-and-crash.
Omega-3s and recovery: Salmon 3–4× per week actively reduces the muscle soreness from exercise. Particularly relevant in the early weeks of returning to training after your ankle injury.
The full picture: Cardio burns fat through a calorie deficit. Strength training builds muscle that raises resting metabolism. This meal plan fuels both — protein to build and preserve muscle, complex carbs for training energy, healthy fats for hormone support. The combination is meaningfully more effective than any single piece alone.
Water and PKD: High water intake is one of the most evidence-backed interventions for slowing PKD progression. Your habit of refilling a 32oz bottle throughout the day is genuinely one of the most impactful things you can do — keep it up regardless of how other dietary details evolve.

Reheating
Fish: 50% microwave power, 90 sec. Or oven 275°F for 10 min covered with foil. High heat = rubbery and overpowering smell.
Chicken + Steak: Splash of water, loosely covered, 90 sec–2 min full power. Steak also excellent cold over quinoa or in a salad — no reheating needed.
Grains + Lentils: Splash of water, covered, 60–90 sec. Fluff grains with a fork after heating.
Containers worth having: Glass 2-compartment meal prep containers — protein and grain stay separate, microwave and dishwasher safe. ~$25–30 for a set of 6 on Amazon. One large container for each grain batch. That's the complete setup.
