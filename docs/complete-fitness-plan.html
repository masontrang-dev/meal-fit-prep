Why This Combination Attacks Belly Fat
Cardio creates the calorie deficit that burns fat — including belly fat. Strength training builds muscle that raises your resting metabolism, so you burn more even on rest days. Together they're significantly more effective than either alone. The core work on Day B builds the underlying muscles so when the fat comes off, definition shows through.
Thursday · ~30 min · At home after treadmill
Chest, Shoulders, Triceps — builds width and pressing strength. Do all A exercises as a superset, rest 2 min, repeat 3×.
Superset A1 + A2 — 3 rounds, then 2 min rest
A1
Dumbbell Floor Press
Chest · Triceps
3 × 10–12
Form Lie on your back, hold DBs at chest, elbows ~45°. Press up, lower until elbows touch the floor. Fully replaces a bench press — limited range actually reduces shoulder strain.

Weeks 1–3 2 sets, find your weight. Weeks 4+ Add third set, increase weight when 12 reps feels easy for 2 sessions in a row.
A2
Overhead Dumbbell Press
Shoulders · Triceps
3 × 10–12
Form Seated or standing, DBs at shoulder height palms forward. Press overhead until arms nearly straight. Brace core — don't arch lower back.

Key cue Keep elbows slightly in front of ears at the top — protects the rotator cuff.
Superset B1 + B2 — 3 rounds, then 2 min rest
B1
Lateral Raises
Side Delts — #1 exercise for shoulder width
3 × 12–15
Form Stand, raise arms out to sides until parallel with floor, slight elbow bend. Go lighter than you think — most people use too much weight and lose the benefit.

Why This is what creates the "broad" shoulder look.
B2
Tricep Overhead Extension
Triceps — 2/3 of upper arm size
3 × 12
Form Hold one DB with both hands overhead. Lower behind head by bending elbows, press back up. Keep elbows pointing forward — don't let them flare wide.
Finisher C1 — 2 sets to failure
C1
Push-Ups (wide then close grip)
Chest · Triceps · Core
2 × max
How to 1 set wide grip (hands outside shoulders), 1 set close grip (hands under chest). Max reps until form breaks — even 5 reps is fine at first.

Track this Note your reps each week. Going from 5 to 20 push-ups over 11 weeks is very achievable and visible.
Sunday · ~30–35 min · At home, no treadmill today
Back, Biceps, Core — improves posture, builds pulling strength, and directly works the midsection.
Superset A1 + A2 — 3 rounds, then 2 min rest
A1
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
Upper Back · Lats · Biceps
3 × 10 each
Form One hand on chair for support, pull DB elbow-to-hip. Lower slowly — the lowering phase is half the work. Keep back flat.

Key cue Think "elbow to hip" not "hand to shoulder." Most effective back exercise with dumbbells alone.
A2
Alternating Dumbbell Curl
Biceps
3 × 10–12 each
Form Stand tall, curl one DB at a time. Rotate palm up as you curl (supinate). Don't swing — if swinging, weight is too heavy.

Week 5+ variation Try hammer curls (palms facing each other) alternating weeks — adds overall arm thickness.
Superset B1 + B2 — 3 rounds, then 2 min rest
B1
Dumbbell Reverse Fly
Rear Delts · Upper Back · Posture
3 × 12–15
Form Hinge forward ~45°, raise arms out to sides with slight elbow bend. Very light weight — these are small muscles. Squeeze shoulder blades at top.

Why it matters Fixes rounded posture from sitting — you'll stand taller and look leaner immediately.
B2
Renegade Row
Back · Core · Stability
3 × 6–8 each
Form Push-up position with hands on DBs. Row one DB to hip while other arm supports. Keep hips square — don't rotate.

Progression Start on knees if needed. Works entire core while training back simultaneously.
Core Circuit C1→C2→C3 — 3 rounds, minimal rest between exercises
C1
Dumbbell Russian Twist
Obliques — Side belly definition
3 × 20
Form Sit with knees bent, lean back slightly. Hold one DB with both hands. Rotate side to side, touching DB to floor = 1 rep each side.

Directly targets the obliques — creates the defined "sides" look when body fat drops.
C2
Dead Bug
Deep Core — Flat stomach foundation
3 × 10 each
Form Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°. Lower opposite arm and leg toward floor while pressing lower back INTO the floor. Return and switch.

Trains the transverse abdominis — the deepest core muscle that "pulls in" the waistline. More effective for belly appearance than crunches.
C3
Plank
Full Core · Anti-sag
3 × 20–60 sec
Form Forearms on floor, body in straight line. Don't let hips sag or pike. Breathe steadily. Start with 20 seconds — quality over duration.

Progress Add 5 seconds per week. Target 60 seconds by Week 11.

Use these treadmill methods based on which phase you're in. All methods burn calories and reduce body fat.
Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3 (Rebuild)
W1–3
Incline Walking
35–45 min · 1–4% incline · Easy pace
3–4×/wk
Pace Comfortable — you can hold a full conversation. This is about rebuilding the habit and protecting the ankle.

Incline progression Week 1: 1–2%. Week 2: 2–3%. Week 3: 3–4%. Never push incline if ankle complains.
Phase 2 — Weeks 4–8 (Build)
W4–6
Walk / Jog Intervals
40–45 min · 2 min walk / 1 min jog
3–4×/wk
How to Walk at 3.0–3.5mph for 2 minutes, then jog at 4.5–5.5mph for 1 minute. Repeat for the duration.

Burns 20–30% more calories than steady-state walking. Ankle permitting — skip jog portions if it flares up.
W6–8
Steep Incline Challenge
45–50 min · 6–8% incline · Moderate pace
1–2×/wk
The 12-3-30 method 12% incline, 3.0 mph, 30 min. Don't hold the handrails — it reduces the calorie burn significantly.

Extremely high calorie burn with zero impact. Great for the Saturday long session.
Phase 3 — Weeks 9–11 (Peak)
W9–11
Run / Walk Sustained
50–55 min · 1:1 walk/jog or steady jog
3–4×/wk
Goal By Week 9–10, aim to jog for 5–10 minutes continuously before walking. By Week 11, see how long you can sustain a comfortable jog.

You'll have built the base — trust it and push a little.

Nutrition = 70% of Your Results
You don't need a strict diet — you need a slight calorie deficit with high protein. This approach preserves muscle while losing fat, which is what creates the lean, defined look by June. No elimination, no obsession — just a few consistent habits.
📊 Daily Targets
  • 1,800–2,000 calories/day
  • 100–130g protein
  • 80–100 oz water
  • 7–8 hours sleep
  • Don't skip meals
✅ Prioritize
  • Protein at every meal
  • Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt
  • Vegetables for volume
  • Fruit as sweet snacks
  • Eat before workouts
↓ Reduce (not eliminate)
  • Liquid calories (soda, alcohol)
  • Late-night snacking
  • Ultra-processed foods
  • Large refined carb portions
  • Mindless eating
